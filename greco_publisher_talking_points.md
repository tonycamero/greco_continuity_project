# Greco Publisher Talking Points

## Core Answer

Yes, the revised edition can travel now. The publisher should not evaluate it as a narrow alternative-money manuscript. It should be evaluated as a timely systems-change book about credit, exchange, sovereignty, local resilience, and trust infrastructure.

The core support set is the continuity plan, the publisher brief, and the Chapter 21 expansion. The market map should serve those documents by quantifying the ecosystem around them, not by turning the project into an exhaustive academic survey.

## Ten Points For Berrett-Koehler

1. The audience has expanded from monetary reform into resilience, sovereignty, commons, regenerative economics, digital trust, and post-growth economics.
2. CBDCs and stablecoins have moved monetary architecture into mainstream policy conversation.
3. Debanking has made access to financial infrastructure a public political question.
4. Mutual credit and barter are active practitioner fields, not purely historical examples.
5. Local currencies and community exchange systems are legible to readers concerned with localism, food systems, and regional resilience.
6. Post-growth and energy-economics readers already understand that money is a claim on real resources; Greco gives them an exchange-system framework.
7. Commons and peer-to-peer networks give the book an intellectual distribution lane beyond conventional economics.
8. AI provenance and decentralized identity make "trust infrastructure" newly mainstream; Greco can be positioned as the economic side of that trust conversation.
9. Chapter 21 can be used as a strategic travel asset for reviewers, podcasts, webinars, and practitioner networks.
10. The question is not whether the whole movement can be mapped perfectly. The question is whether enough live channels exist to support discovery. They do.

## Recommended Publisher Framing

This is a book about how communities regain exchange capacity when official money, bank credit, platform governance, and institutional trust become unstable. Greco's work gives readers a language for alternatives that are practical, historically grounded, and less speculative than crypto-token narratives.

## What To Avoid

- Do not lead with "alternative monetary reform" as the category.
- Do not overclaim mass-market inevitability.
- Do not make the package sound ideological.
- Do not bury the practical mutual credit and local resilience angles under academic history.
- Do not try to prove every movement lineage before making the travel argument.

## Best One-Sentence Pitch

Greco's revised edition arrives at the moment when money, credit, digital trust, and local resilience have converged into one public question: who gets to create the means of exchange, and under what governance?
