## Purpose

These are the core project documents. Any market research, contact mapping, or publisher-facing argument should support this source hierarchy rather than drifting into a broad academic movement map.

## Publisher Confidence Frame

The publisher does not need a perfect map of the entire movement. The publisher needs confidence that the book has enough live audience pathways to travel.

The working claim is:

> Since Thomas first published the book, the audience around these issues has expanded substantially beyond monetary reform into regenerative economics, local resilience, bioregionalism, commons governance, digital trust systems, and related fields. The current work is to quantify that ecosystem.

## How The Pieces Fit

1. The continuity plan defines the mission.
2. The publisher brief defines the publishing case.
3. Chapter 21 supplies the contemporary hook.
4. The market map supplies evidence that active audiences and channels already exist.
5. The contact CSV turns that evidence into an outreach path.

## Editorial Guardrails

- Keep the argument sober and evidence-based.
- Lead with travel-readiness, not total completeness.
- Treat Chapter 21 as the most useful circulation asset.
- Separate core audiences from bridge audiences.
- Use audience quantification to support the publisher decision, not to prove every lineage.
- Avoid ideological framing where a business-facing publishing argument is stronger.

## Best Current Formulation

This revised edition can travel because it meets multiple active conversations at once: monetary reform, local resilience, mutual credit, commons governance, post-growth economics, financial sovereignty, and digital trust. Greco's distinctive contribution is that he does not merely critique the existing system. He offers a practical framework for rebuilding exchange around reciprocal obligation, redeemable claims, productive capacity, local stewardship, and trust.
