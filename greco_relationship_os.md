# Greco Relationship OS

## Purpose

The Greco Continuity Project should become an execution workhorse without becoming an outreach machine.

The operating principle is:

> Manage 100 relationships, not 100 emails.

The scarce resource is not message-sending capacity. The scarce resource is judgment: knowing which relationship to advance, why now, with what ask, and what risk.

## Philosophy

This system is designed to help Tony and Codex act like a chief-of-staff function for the Greco Continuity Project.

Codex should help with:

- relationship intelligence;
- context preparation;
- draft generation;
- conversation tracking;
- next-action prioritization;
- strategic sequencing.

Codex should not automatically blast messages to high-value contacts.

For Tier 1 relationships, every outbound message should remain human-approved.

## Core Question

Every morning, the system should answer:

> What is the single highest-leverage conversation I should advance today?

Not:

> Who should I email today?

One optimizes activity. The other optimizes outcomes.

## Operating Layers

### Layer 1: Relationship Intelligence

For every person or organization, maintain:

- who they are;
- why they matter;
- relevant recent work;
- relationship stage;
- current priority;
- last interaction;
- next best action;
- risk or sensitivity;
- strongest overlap with Greco / Chapter 21;
- best possible ask.

### Layer 2: Context Generation

Before outreach, Codex should prepare:

- who this person is;
- why they should care;
- what their current public work suggests;
- what Greco / Chapter 21 overlaps with;
- what the ask is;
- what the relationship risk is;
- what not to say;
- what a successful next step would look like.

### Layer 3: Drafting

Codex drafts:

- email;
- LinkedIn message;
- follow-up;
- thank-you;
- review request;
- interview pitch;
- briefing note;
- intro request.

Human approval is required before sending, especially for:

- publishers;
- Schumacher;
- reviewers;
- Tier 1 cultural translators;
- universe-tier contacts.

### Layer 4: Conversation Tracking

Every response should be logged as:

- relationship;
- date;
- signal;
- opportunity;
- concern;
- next action;
- owner;
- deadline.

The goal is compounding relationship intelligence.

## Relationship Stages

| Stage | Meaning | Action posture |
|---|---|---|
| Research | Need context before contact. | Gather recent work and relevance. |
| Warm Path Needed | Contact should route through intro or shared node. | Identify bridge person. |
| Draft Ready | Context is sufficient for a message draft. | Draft for human approval. |
| Contacted | Outreach sent. | Wait, track, and avoid over-following. |
| Responded | Contact replied. | Interpret signal and propose next action. |
| Active Conversation | Relationship is alive. | Prepare briefings and follow-through. |
| Opportunity | Concrete ask or collaboration exists. | Move carefully and document commitments. |
| Dormant | No current action. | Revisit later with new reason. |

## Priority Rules

Priority is not just fame.

Score relationships by:

- strategic fit;
- timing;
- relationship warmth;
- category-making potential;
- publisher relevance;
- risk of bad outreach;
- current opportunity window.

## Safety Rules

- Do not automate first contact to Tier 1 relationships.
- Do not use generic outreach language.
- Do not lead with "monetary reform" for younger-adjacent audiences.
- Do not overclaim Greco as the origin of movements he did not directly create.
- Do not ask for endorsement before establishing resonance.
- Do not contact universe-tier names without a sharp cultural hook.
- Always separate exploratory conversation from transactional ask.

## Daily Operating Ritual

Each morning:

1. Review the dashboard.
2. Identify the highest-leverage relationship to advance.
3. Generate a relationship brief.
4. Decide the next best action.
5. Draft if appropriate.
6. Log outcome.
