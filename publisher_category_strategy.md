# Publisher Category Strategy

## Acquisitions Editor Question

If Thomas Greco's revised edition were being introduced for the first time in 2026, where should it sit?

## Category Evaluation

| Category | Strength | Weakness | Publisher usefulness |
|---|---|---|---|
| Monetary Reform | Accurate historically; existing Greco base. | Small, old category; limits growth. | Use as root, not primary shelf. |
| Local Currencies | Concrete and practical. | Too narrow; misses AI, digital trust, creator economy, public goods. | Use as proof point. |
| Crypto / Web3 | Large, young, noisy. | Tribal, speculative, reputational risk. | Avoid as main category. |
| Regenerative Economics | Established, positive, publisher-legible. | Broad; may bury exchange architecture. | Strong parent shelf. |
| Commons Governance | Good intellectual fit. | Too academic for mass travel. | Useful secondary frame. |
| Community Wealth | Accessible and practical. | Less future-facing; less digital. | Strong business/social-change shelf. |
| Platform Cooperativism | Strong digital ownership fit. | Niche and labor-platform specific. | Good outreach lane, not shelf. |
| Trust Infrastructure | Very timely with AI and identity. | Sounds technical, less book-market friendly. | Good subtitle or Chapter 21 frame. |
| Regenerative Coordination Economy | Best category-creation potential. | Not established; must be explained. | Best strategic umbrella if paired with known shelves. |

## Recommendation

Primary category narrative:

> Regenerative Coordination Economy

Publisher-facing shelf pairing:

> Regenerative Economics / Community Wealth / Technology & Society

Chapter 21 conceptual frame:

> Trust-Mediated Exchange

## Why This Works

Berrett-Koehler does not need the term to already dominate bookstores. It needs a clear reason the book can travel across live communities. "Regenerative Coordination Economy" does that by connecting:

- regenerative economics;
- public goods funding;
- creator ownership;
- community wealth;
- local resilience;
- platform cooperativism;
- digital identity;
- AI governance;
- commons governance;
- mutual credit.

## Recommended Publisher Positioning

> The revised edition is not only a monetary reform book. It is a foundational text for a new economics of trust, contribution, and community value flow. Chapter 21 bridges Greco's Credit Commons work with the emerging Regenerative Coordination Economy: the world of creators, builders, open-source communities, regenerative networks, local-first software, public goods funders, and AI governance thinkers trying to coordinate value without extraction.

## One-Sentence Shelf Answer

Put it on the shelf where regenerative economics meets technology, trust, and community wealth.

## Falsification Result

The hypothesis survives, but with a constraint:

- "Regenerative Coordination Economy" is strong as a category-creation narrative.
- It is weak as a standalone established bookstore category.
- Therefore it should be used as the future-facing category, while the publisher metadata should remain anchored in established categories: economics, technology and society, community wealth, social change, and regenerative economics.
