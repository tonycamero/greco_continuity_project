# Regenerative Coordination Economy

## Working Definition

The **Regenerative Coordination Economy** is the emerging field of people, communities, creators, builders, institutions, and networks trying to coordinate trust, contribution, governance, funding, identity, reputation, and value flow without surrendering control to extractive platforms, centralized institutions, or speculative token systems.

It is not simply a new name for monetary reform. It is a wider category in which monetary reform becomes one necessary layer inside a larger problem: how communities coordinate value and obligation over time.

## Short Definition

The Regenerative Coordination Economy is the economy of communities building non-extractive systems for trust, contribution, governance, and value flow.

## Why This Category Matters For Greco

Thomas Greco spent decades asking how communities should exchange value. The next generation is increasingly concerned with how communities should coordinate.

Those questions converge. Community coordination eventually requires exchange, contribution recognition, obligation tracking, reputation, trust, and value flow. That is Greco territory, but the younger market rarely enters through "money."

## Core Questions

1. How do communities recognize contribution?
2. How do communities build and maintain trust?
3. How do communities govern shared resources?
4. How do communities exchange value without extraction?
5. How do communities maintain sovereignty over identity, data, relationships, and reputation?
6. How do communities fund public goods and common infrastructure?

8. How do AI agents participate in exchange while remaining accountable to human stewardship?

## Historical Roots

### Schumacher

E. F. Schumacher's legacy frames economics around human scale, local production, and moral limits. The Schumacher Center continues to operate as a new economics institution with local currency programming and BerkShares history. Source: https://centerforneweconomics.org/apply/local-currencies-program/

### Greco

Greco's work supplies the exchange layer: reciprocal exchange, mutual credit, credit clearing, reclaiming the Credit Commons, and honest claims on real value. His Beyond Money site frames him as an authority on moneyless exchange systems, community currencies, and financial innovation. Source: https://beyondmoney.net/about/

### Commons

The commons tradition supplies the governance and shared-resource frame. P2P Foundation's web ecosystem is built around peer production, commons, and governance. Source: https://p2pfoundation.net/home-page/the-p2p-foundations-web-ecosystem

### Mutual Credit

Mutual credit supplies a practical accounting system for trusted networks. Credit Commons Society defines mutual credit as accounting for exchange within a trusted network and points to real-world examples such as IRTA, Sardex, and Grassroots Economics. Source: https://creditcommonssociety.org/mutual-credit/

### Bioregionalism

Bioregionalism and transition movements supply the local resilience frame. Transition Network describes a movement spanning 67 countries. Source: https://transitionnetwork.org/about-the-movement/what-is-transition/characteristics/

## Modern Drivers

### AI

AI makes intelligence abundant, but not trust. As more decisions are automated, communities need identity, authority, reputation, and obligation frameworks. Chapter 21's constitutional stewardship argument becomes directly relevant.

### Digital Trust

The Content Authenticity Initiative reports a community of more than 5,000 members around content provenance and C2PA. This shows trust infrastructure becoming mainstream. Source: https://contentauthenticity.org/our-members

### Decentralized Identity

The Decentralized Identity Foundation describes members building standards and open-source components for interoperable decentralized identity. Source: https://identity.foundation/join/

### Local-First Software

Ink & Switch defines local-first software around collaboration and ownership despite the cloud. Local-First Conf 2025 described a 300-attendee event for a rapidly growing community. Sources: https://www.inkandswitch.com/essay/local-first/ and https://www.localfirstconf.com/local-first-conf-2025

### Platform Extraction

Creators increasingly want ownership of audience, products, and revenue. Kajabi's 2025 report says creators are moving from influencers to entrepreneurs and that 55% cite audience ownership as the most important success factor. Sources: https://kajabi.com/state-of-creator-commerce-25 and https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20250417375846/en/Kajabis-2025-State-of-Creator-Commerce-Report-Unveils-Major-Shift-in-the-Creator-Economy-as-Creators-Embrace-an-Entrepreneurial-Mindset

### Creator Economies

The creator economy is a mass-market trust and relationship problem. Creators ask how they own audiences, monetize communities, and escape algorithmic dependence. Patreon crossed a reported $10B creator payment milestone with more than 25M paid memberships. Source: https://www.axios.com/2025/08/05/patreon-10-billion-creator-economy-ai

### Public Goods Funding

Funding the Commons frames its work as developing coordination infrastructure for public goods: funding mechanisms, governance systems, and programmable tools. Source: https://www.fundingthecommons.io/

### Regenerative Web3 / ReFi

Greenpill Network explicitly connects community currency, mutual aid, local governance, public goods, open source, AI and automation, impact tracking, and regenerative crypto. Source: https://greenpill.network/

## Category Strength

Strong:

- The underlying problem is real.
- The adjacent fields are active.
- The younger-generation language is meaningfully different from Greco's historical category.
- Chapter 21 naturally bridges money, digital trust, AI, governance, and redeemable claims.

Weak / unproven:

- "Regenerative Coordination Economy" is not yet an established shelf label.
- The category may need a simpler public-facing subtitle.
- Some adjacent audiences may resist monetary language.
- Publishers may need the category paired with more familiar shelves: economics, technology, society, community wealth, and regenerative economics.

## Recommended Use

Use **Regenerative Coordination Economy** as the category-creation umbrella, but do not expect it to carry the whole pitch alone. Pair it with:

> "A new economics of trust, contribution, and community value flow."
