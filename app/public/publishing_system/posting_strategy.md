# Posting Strategy

## Purpose

This system exists to make the Greco Continuity Project visible as a timely category-creation effort, not as a niche monetary reform campaign.

The public work should do three things:

1. Name the category: Regenerative Coordination Economy.
2. Teach the mechanism: Trust-Mediated Exchange.
3. Create reasons for specific people to engage with Chapter 21 and the revised edition.

## Voice

Clear, category-forming, and sober.

Avoid:

- hype;
- crypto boosterism;
- ideological purity tests;
- claiming Greco invented every adjacent movement;
- leading with "monetary reform" for younger audiences;
- asking for endorsement too early.

Prefer:

- "This is the missing exchange layer."
- "Community without exchange is fragile."
- "Trust is becoming economic infrastructure."
- "Money is not just a thing. It is a claim, a relationship, and a governance problem."
- "The next economic question is coordination."

## Cadence

Publish daily.

Recommended rhythm:

| Day Type | Platform | Function |
|---|---|---|
| Category claim | LinkedIn | Make the market legible. |
| Teaching thread | X | Explain one concept in 6-10 posts. |
| Bridge post | LinkedIn | Connect Greco to an active adjacent market. |
| Debate thread | X | Distinguish this from crypto, barter, fintech, or ideology. |
| Relationship post | LinkedIn | Create a reason to notify specific contacts. |

## Notification Rule

Each public post gets 3-8 suggested notifications.

Notifications should be framed as:

- "This made me think of your work on..."
- "I am developing this bridge between Greco and..."
- "I would value your perspective if this touches your lane."

Do not mass-message.

Do not ask for shares by default.

Do not contact universe-tier names unless the hook is unusually sharp.

## Weekly Review

Every 7 posts, review:

- which category phrases felt strongest;
- which contacts were worth notifying;
- which posts produced replies, saves, comments, or follow-up reasons;
- whether the next week should lean more toward publishers, creators, public goods, local resilience, or technical trust infrastructure.
