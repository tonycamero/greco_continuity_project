# Greco Ecosystem Market Map

## Editorial Standard

This package is optimized for publisher confidence, not exhaustive academic completeness. The question is:

> Can this revised edition travel now?

The answer should be judged by whether there is enough current audience, channel, reviewer, interview, and partner evidence to support a publishing decision. It is not necessary to map the entire alternative-money movement perfectly.

## Core Source Set

This market map should be read as a support document for three core project files:

1. `The Greco Continuity Project.docx` - the strategic continuity plan. It establishes the mission: complete, publish, preserve, extend, and operationalize Thomas H. Greco Jr.'s work so it remains active in the emerging economic, technological, and social landscape.
2. `THE END OF MONEY 2026 Revised Edition-Publisher Brief.docx` - the publisher-facing case. It frames the revised edition as timely because debt, crypto, stablecoins, CBDCs, AI, digital identity, localization, and resilience have made Greco's questions immediate.
3. `Chapter 21 Expansion FINAL.docx` - the current travel asset. It connects Greco's long-standing monetary argument to digital scarcity, blockchain as verification, AI-assisted reciprocal exchange, constitutional stewardship, redeemable claims on real value, and gradual transition strategy.

The market research should not replace these documents. It should quantify the audience ecosystem around them.

## Working Market Claim

Since Thomas first published the book, the audience around these issues has expanded substantially beyond monetary reform into regenerative economics, local resilience, bioregionalism, commons governance, digital trust systems, and related fields. The current task is to quantify that ecosystem well enough to support publisher confidence.

## Executive Summary

Thomas H. Greco Jr.'s revised edition can plausibly travel now because the book no longer sits only inside the narrow alternative monetary reform audience. Its strongest contemporary market is an overlap of five active publics:

1. Local resilience and regenerative economics readers.
2. Mutual credit, barter, local currency, and cooperative exchange practitioners.
3. Commons, peer-to-peer, and decentralized governance networks.
4. Sovereignty, CBDC, debanking, and stablecoin-skeptical audiences.
5. AI provenance, decentralized identity, and digital trust thinkers.

The publisher-facing opportunity is not that every reader will arrive through Greco's original monetary-reform lane. The opportunity is that multiple present-day conversations are now asking questions Greco has long addressed: Who controls credit? Can communities create exchange capacity without bank debt? What happens when official money, platform governance, financial access, and digital trust become political?

## Market Thesis

The revised edition is no longer only for alternative monetary reformers. It now sits at the intersection of monetary instability, local resilience, regenerative economics, post-growth economics, crypto and stablecoin skepticism, AI governance, and decentralized trust systems. The book can travel if positioned as a practical, historically grounded theory of exchange, credit, and community-scale monetary sovereignty rather than as a niche monetary-reform text.

## Contact And Channel Notes

This is a publisher-confidence scan. It intentionally avoids personal emails unless publicly listed as professional contact points. It also treats social follower counts as optional, not essential. For the publisher decision, the strongest proof is the existence of multiple live channels with clear editorial fit, not a perfect follower census.

## Publisher Talking Points

1. The audience for this book has expanded from monetary reform into resilience, sovereignty, commons, regenerative economics, and digital trust.
2. The revised edition is arriving when CBDCs, stablecoins, debanking, AI provenance, debt saturation, and institutional distrust have made Greco's core questions newly mainstream.
3. The book can be pitched as a grounded, pre-hype alternative to both bank-controlled money and speculative crypto.
4. The strongest distribution path is not a single mass audience; it is a coalition of reachable niche audiences with overlapping concern.
5. Mutual credit, barter, local currency, and community exchange are not historical curiosities; they remain active practitioner fields.
6. Greco's strongest current advantage is that he connects history, theory, and practical exchange architecture.
7. The book should not be sold as ideology. It should be sold as a serious guide to exchange capacity, trust, and local economic agency.
8. Chapter 21 can function as a travel piece: a review copy, webinar basis, podcast hook, and discussion excerpt.
9. Berrett-Koehler's positive-change and systems-change audience is a better fit if the pitch foregrounds resilience, governance, and community wealth rather than only monetary reform.
10. The publisher question is not whether the entire movement can be mapped; it is whether there are enough active channels to support discovery. The evidence says yes.
